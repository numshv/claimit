# Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF)

## 1. Description
TANF provides temporary cash assistance and work opportunities to low-income families with dependent children to help them achieve economic self-sufficiency.

## 2. Core Eligibility Requirements
- **Age/Family Status:** You MUST have a child under age 18, be pregnant, or be 18 or younger and the head of your household.
- **Citizenship:** U.S. citizens and qualified eligible aliens.
- **Income Limit:** Must be underemployed, unemployed, or about to become unemployed with very low income (limits vary drastically by state).
- **Employment Status:** Adult recipients are generally required to participate in work activities for a minimum number of hours per week.

## 3. Exclusions and Conflicts (CRITICAL)
- **Who cannot apply:** Single adults or couples WITHOUT dependent children. 
- **Time Limits:** You cannot receive TANF assistance for more than 60 months (5 years) in your lifetime (some states have even shorter limits).

## 4. Benefits (Pros)
- Monthly cash assistance to help pay for food, shelter, utilities, and expenses other than medical.
- Access to job preparation, work assistance, and childcare support.

## 5. Application Steps & Required Documents
- **Step 1:** Find your state or tribal TANF program office to apply, as there is no national TANF application.
- **Step 2:** Agree to a Personal Responsibility Plan (often requiring job searches or training).
- **Documents Needed:** 
  - Proof of relationship to the children (Birth Certificates).
  - Social Security Numbers.
  - Proof of all income and assets.
