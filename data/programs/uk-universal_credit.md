# Universal Credit

## 1. Description
Universal Credit is a single monthly payment to help with your living costs. It replaces six legacy benefits, rolling them into one unified system. You may be able to get it if you're on a low income, out of work, or cannot work.

## 2. Core Eligibility Requirements
- **Location:** Must live in the UK.
- **Age:** 18 or over (with some exceptions for 16-17 year olds), and under State Pension age.
- **Resources:** You and your partner must have less than £16,000 in money, savings, and investments combined.

## 3. Exclusions and Conflicts (CRITICAL)
- **Who cannot apply:** Full-time students (unless they meet strict exemptions like having children or a severe disability). Anyone with over £16,000 in savings.
- **Program Conflicts:** Replaces and cannot be claimed simultaneously with legacy benefits like Housing Benefit, Income Support, or Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA).

## 4. Benefits (Pros)
- A standard monthly allowance.
- Potential extra payments if you have children, have a disability or health condition preventing work, or need help paying your rent.

## 5. Application Steps & Required Documents
- **Step 1:** Apply entirely online via GOV.UK.
- **Step 2:** Attend an interview with a work coach at your local Jobcentre Plus (often within a few days of applying).
- **Documents Needed:** Bank details, email address, proof of identity (Passport, Driving Licence), details of income and housing costs (tenancy agreement).
