# Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)

## 1. Description
SNAP provides low-income individuals and families with funds to purchase food at grocery stores, farmers' markets, and online via an Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) card.

## 2. Core Eligibility Requirements
- **Age:** No strict age limit.
- **Citizenship:** Must be a U.S. citizen or a qualified non-citizen (e.g., refugees, or lawful permanent residents for at least 5 years).
- **Income Limit:** Gross monthly household income must be at or below 130% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL).
- **Employment Status:** Able-Bodied Adults Without Dependents (ABAWDs) ages 18-52 must work or volunteer at least 80 hours a month to get SNAP for more than 3 months.
- **Resources:** Households can have up to $2,750 in a bank account, or $4,250 if a member is 60+ or disabled.

## 3. Exclusions and Conflicts (CRITICAL)
- **Who cannot apply:** Undocumented immigrants, workers currently on strike, and most college students attending more than half-time (unless they work 20 hours/week).
- **Program Conflicts:** Cannot receive SNAP and Tribal food assistance (FDPIR) in the same month.

## 4. Benefits (Pros)
- Monthly funds loaded onto an EBT card for groceries.
- Automatically qualifies children in the household for free school meals.

## 5. Application Steps & Required Documents
- **Step 1:** Apply online through your specific state's Department of Social Services portal.
- **Step 2:** Complete a mandatory eligibility interview (phone or in-person).
- **Documents Needed:** 
  - Proof of identity (Driver's License, State ID).
  - Social Security Numbers (SSN) for everyone applying.
  - Proof of income (pay stubs) and residence (utility bill).
