# Section 8 (Housing Choice Voucher Program)

## 1. Description
The housing choice voucher program is the federal government's major program for assisting very low-income families, the elderly, and the disabled to afford decent, safe, and sanitary housing in the private market.

## 2. Core Eligibility Requirements
- **Income Limit:** Household income may not exceed 50% of the median income for the county or metropolitan area in which the family chooses to live. (By law, 75% of vouchers must go to those whose incomes do not exceed 30% of the area median income).
- **Citizenship:** Limited to US citizens and specified categories of non-citizens.
- **Family Status:** Must meet HUD's definition of a family, elderly, or disabled individual.

## 3. Exclusions and Conflicts (CRITICAL)
- **Who cannot apply:** Individuals who have been evicted from public housing in the last 3 years for drug-related criminal activity. Registered lifetime sex offenders are strictly prohibited.
- **Cons/Drawbacks:** EXTREMELY long waitlists. Being eligible does not mean you get a voucher immediately; it can take years.

## 4. Benefits (Pros)
- You find your own housing (single-family homes, townhouses, apartments) as long as the landlord agrees to the program.
- You generally pay 30% of your monthly adjusted gross income for rent and utilities, and the voucher covers the rest.

## 5. Application Steps & Required Documents
- **Step 1:** Apply through your local Public Housing Agency (PHA). 
- **Step 2:** If accepted, you will be placed on a waiting list.
- **Documents Needed:** 
  - Complete background check authorization.
  - Extensive proof of income, assets, and bank statements.
  - Birth certificates and SSNs for all family members.---
