# Medicaid

## 1. Description
Medicaid provides free or low-cost health coverage to millions of Americans, including eligible low-income adults, children, pregnant women, elderly adults, and people with disabilities.

## 2. Core Eligibility Requirements
- **Age:** Available to all ages, but specific programs target children (CHIP) and the elderly.
- **Citizenship:** Must be a U.S. citizen or a qualified non-citizen.
- **Income Limit:** In states that expanded Medicaid, adults with incomes up to 138% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL) qualify. In non-expansion states, rules are much stricter and often require being pregnant, disabled, or a parent.
- **Household:** Eligibility is heavily based on Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) and household size.

## 3. Exclusions and Conflicts (CRITICAL)
- **Who cannot apply:** Undocumented immigrants (except for emergency medical services). Adults without dependents in states that did NOT expand Medicaid often do not qualify regardless of income.
- **Program Conflicts:** You can have both Medicare and Medicaid (Dual Eligible), but Medicaid is always the "payer of last resort".

## 4. Benefits (Pros)
- Covers doctor visits, hospital stays, long-term medical care, preventative care, and prescriptions.
- Extremely low or zero out-of-pocket costs.

## 5. Application Steps & Required Documents
- **Step 1:** Apply via HealthCare.gov or directly through your state's Medicaid agency.
- **Step 2:** If applying via HealthCare.gov, the system will automatically route your application to your state agency if you qualify.
- **Documents Needed:** 
  - Proof of citizenship/immigration status.
  - Tax returns or W-2s to prove MAGI.
  - Proof of state residency.
