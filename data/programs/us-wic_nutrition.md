# Women, Infants, and Children (WIC)

## 1. Description
WIC provides federal grants to states for supplemental foods, health care referrals, and nutrition education for low-income pregnant, breastfeeding, and non-breastfeeding postpartum women, and to infants and children up to age 5.

## 2. Core Eligibility Requirements
- **Age/Category:** Must be a pregnant woman, a woman up to 6 months postpartum, a breastfeeding woman up to 1 year postpartum, an infant, or a child under 5 years old.
- **Income Limit:** Gross income must fall at or below 185% of the U.S. Poverty Income Guidelines.
- **Nutritional Risk:** Must be seen by a health professional to determine that the individual is at "nutritional risk" (e.g., anemia, underweight, poor diet).

## 3. Exclusions and Conflicts (CRITICAL)
- **Who cannot apply:** Children aged 5 and older. Men cannot receive WIC benefits for themselves, but fathers/guardians CAN apply for their eligible infants and children.
- **Program Synergies:** If you already receive SNAP, Medicaid, or TANF, you automatically meet the income eligibility requirement for WIC.

## 4. Benefits (Pros)
- Specific nutritious foods (milk, eggs, cheese, infant formula, fruits, vegetables).
- Free nutrition education and breastfeeding support.
- Referrals to pediatricians and healthcare.

## 5. Application Steps & Required Documents
- **Step 1:** Contact your state or local WIC agency to set up an appointment.
- **Step 2:** Attend a WIC certification appointment which includes a health and diet screening.
- **Documents Needed:** 
  - Proof of identity for yourself and your child.
  - Proof of pregnancy (if applicable).
  - Proof of income or proof of SNAP/Medicaid enrollment.
