# Child Benefit

## 1. Description
Child Benefit is a regular payment given to anyone raising a child. It is paid every 4 weeks and there is no limit to how many children you can claim for.

## 2. Core Eligibility Requirements
- **Responsibility:** You must be responsible for a child under 16, or under 20 if they stay in approved full-time education or training.
- **Income Context:** There is no strict income limit to claim. However, if you or your partner have an individual income over £60,000, you will have to pay the High Income Child Benefit Charge (a tax).

## 3. Exclusions and Conflicts (CRITICAL)
- **Who cannot apply:** Generally, you cannot claim if your child is in hospital or residential care for more than 12 weeks, or in prison. Only one person can claim Child Benefit for a specific child.
- **Immigration Status:** Subject to immigration control; usually cannot claim if you do not have the right to reside in the UK.

## 4. Benefits (Pros)
- Weekly rate of £25.60 for the eldest or only child, and £16.95 for each additional child.
- Provides National Insurance credits which count towards your State Pension.

## 5. Application Steps & Required Documents
- **Step 1:** Claim online via GOV.UK or by sending a filled CH2 form by post.
- **Documents Needed:** Child's original birth certificate or adoption certificate. Your National Insurance number.
